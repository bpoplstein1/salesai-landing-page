the problem

AI is stuck at scattered experiments that never become how you operate.

Everyone is prompting on their own and getting their own results. There's no single source of truth for agents to act on, and no shared way to keep a human in the loop where it counts.

Any of the three ways below moves you out of that.

proof

I don't advise on this.
I operate it.

I run product at an AI company on top of a fleet of agents I built myself. My thinking lives in one place, and the agents read it: they keep every tool my team uses in sync, turn decisions into tickets, keep the docs current, and even draft my published writing from the week's real work. I spend my time on judgment instead of logistics. When I install this for a company, I'm not handing over a theory. I'm transferring the system I depend on every day, including the parts I had to fix the hard way.

// what broke

Early on I gave a sync agent too much autonomy, and it confidently pushed half-formed notes to a surface my whole team reads as truth. Later, a different agent quietly edited a live shared document nobody asked it to touch. Nothing it wrote was even wrong, which is the unsettling part.

The rule I now build everything on, and install for every client: an agent never writes to a trusted, shared surface without explicit permission. It can draft, it can stage, it can suggest. It cannot silently change a thing the team treats as canonical.

That is the difference between an AI demo and an AI system in production. Knowing where to put the gates is most of the job.

  • A decision written once now propagates to every tool automatically. The copy-paste tax is gone.
  • The wiki and roadmap stay current without anyone maintaining them by hand.
  • I walk into planning with an agent-drafted breakdown to react to, instead of a blank page to fill.
  • Agents move fast where it's cheap and pause for a human where it's expensive, so nothing canonical gets silently changed.
  • My hours moved off logistics and onto judgment: what to build, why, and what to say no to.
